Another good turnout for Women Who Code. This time with a Q&A with Guido van Rossum. He looked into the crowd and declared it the biggest gathering of female geeks he's ever seen. Which I have mixed feelings about. On the one hand, great that we've got this community of coders. On the other hand, why is it NOT commonplace to see such gatherings at a size comparable to this?

Answering a question about what he would do differently if he were starting Python from scratch, Guido got a big gasp from the audience when he suggested that one would be using curly braces instead of whitespace. Whoa. I wish.

It also turns out he's quite a fan of Haskell; enough to lovingly criticize the heck out of it.
