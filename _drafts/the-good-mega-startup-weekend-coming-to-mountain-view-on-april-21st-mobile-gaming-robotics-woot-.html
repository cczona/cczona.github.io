The good: Mega Startup Weekend coming to Mountain View on April 21st! Mobile, gaming, robotics, woot! Make the products happen, y'all.

The bad: recruitment problem in the current lineup of organizers, speakers, mentors, judges. http://mega.startupweekend.org/ As best I can tell, just 2 of those 26 are women. For an event that's intended to foster new leadership and startup activity, of all things. (Yes, I've contacted them, and specifically asked what they're doing to fix this. Please add your voice too. http://mega.startupweekend.org/contact/ Organizers need to know how many people notice, and care about, such issues)

The ugly: the weekend is co-sponsored by the otherwise terrific Women 2.0. At the Women 2.0 Startup Weekend in December, women were everywhere: developers, entrepreneurs, speakers, mentors, organizers, target markets... There's no shortage of smart women to draw on. Women 2.0 and the other sponsors should have insisted that the organizers dig deeper into the well before announcing.

Who else is underrepresented at this event, by the way? Doesn't seem to me that blacks leaders in the field are visible either. C'mon, industry, we can and should do better.
