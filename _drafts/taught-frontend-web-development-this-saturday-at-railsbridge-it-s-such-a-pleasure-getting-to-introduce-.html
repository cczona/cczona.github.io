Taught frontend web development this Saturday at Railsbridge. It's such a pleasure getting to introduce total n00bs to this stuff. They asked really insightful questions, which was exciting in itself.

For me, it was good to be challenged to consider these topics through beginners' eyes. Wow is the syntax of HTML vs CSS vs JavaScript tricky when they're all coming at a learner at once; their first encounter altogether with markup or coding. It's an ambitious day, which includes forays into git, bash commandline, SSH, and GitHub.

In the teachers' debriefing session afterward, we had a fascinating discussion about how the different class levels related to the curriculum's topics, and how to keep improving breadth and pacing. I hear one of the advanced classes worked on building a Twitter clone.

Just days left to finish prepping the Ruby on Rails backend Railsbridge workshop this Friday/Saturday (big thank you, +Twitter).

Oh, and we may have snazzy new Railsbridge sticker designs to hand out this weekend...
