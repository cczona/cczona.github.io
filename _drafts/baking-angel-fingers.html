Off to a cookie swap/birthday party for a friend. My contribution is adapted from grandma’s perennial Christmas classic, Angel Fingers:


1/2 c. butter, softened
3 tbsp powdered sugar
1/2 tsp vanilla powder
1/4 tsp cinnamon
pinch of nutmeg
zest of 1 lemon, finely minced
1/8 tsb almond extract
1 c. cake flour
1 c. nuts, chopped finely (walnuts, almonds, pistaschios, or pecans are excellent)
lots of extra powdered sugar

Cream the butter until fluffy. Add sugar. Cream more.

Add vanilla, cinnamon, nutmeg, & almond extract. Cream more.

Slowly sprinkle about half the zest into the flour, stirring well to separate and coat. Gradually incorporate the flour/zest into the butter mixture.

Fold in nuts.

The mixture should be rather dry and crumbly. If necessary add a little more flour. (Don’t worry about being exact. The margin for error on the cake flour is pretty generous. I’ve had these cookies come out well even with nearly twice this much flour.)

Refrigerate 15-30 minutes to firm up the butter a bit. Preheat oven to 350.

Shape dough into crescents, roughly finger-sized. Place on greased baking sheet or parchment-lined sheet. Cook ~20mins just until set with light golden edges.

Cool briefly, about 5 minutes. While still warm, roll in bowl of powdered sugar to coat. Cool thoroughly before storing.

For a prettier finish, you can roll the cookies in powdered sugar a second time after they’ve completely cooled. (For an extra zing, you can incorporate the leftover zest into the sugar before doing the second coat!)
